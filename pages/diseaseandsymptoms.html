1) Common Symptoms in Cows

These symptoms may indicate illness in cows:

  • Fever/high body temperature
  • Loss of appetite
  • Low water intake
  • Reduced milk production
  • Weakness/tiredness
  • Diarrhea/loose motion
  • Coughing/nasal discharge
  • Swelling in udder or body parts
  • Lameness/difficulty walking
  • Weight loss
  • Skin patches
  • Abnormal milk (watery, thick, blood)

2) Major causes of Cow Diseases

  1. Viral infections (example : FMD)
  2. Bacterial Infections (example : Mastitis, Brucellosis)
  3. Parasites (ticks, worms)
  4. Unhygenic shed and dirty water
  5. Sudden change in feed
  6. Nutritional deficiency (calcium, minerals)
  7. Extreme weather conditions

4) Seasonal Diseases :

Rainy Season

  • Foot rot
  • Tick fever
  • Worm infection

Winter Season

  • Pneumonia
  • Cold stress

Summer Season

  • Heat stroke
  • Dehydration

5) Do's and Dont's (CowCare Hub)

Do's

  • Provide clean drinking water
  • Keep shed clean and dry
  • Give balanced feed and minerals
  • Vaccinate cows regularly
  • Check udder health daily

Dont's

  • Do not keep sick cows with healthy cows
  • Do not suddenly change cow diet
  • Do not ignore fever and diarrhea
  • Do not use dirty milking equipment

6) Emergency Warning Signs

Contact a veterinarian immediately if the cow shows:

  • Cannot stand / unconscious
  • Continuous hig fever
  • Severe breathing problem
  • Blood in stool or urine
  • Severe dehydration
  • Sudden stop milk production
