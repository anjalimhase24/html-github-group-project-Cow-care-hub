Disease and Symptoms

Healthy cows give better milk, live longer, and reduce loss for farmers. Early identification of diseases helps in timely treatment and prevents spread to other animals.


1) Common Symptoms in Cows

These symptoms may indicate illness in cows:

  • Fever/high body temperature
  • Loss of appetite
  • Low water intake
  • Reduced milk production
  • Weakness/tiredness
  • Diarrhea/loose motion
  • Coughing/nasal discharge
  • Swelling in udder or body parts
  • Lameness/difficulty walking
  • Weight loss
  • Skin patches
  • Abnormal milk (watery, thick, blood)

2) Major Causes of Cow Diseases

Sr. No. Cause Type Description Examples
1 Viral Infections Viral infections spread rapidly among cattle through direct contact, air, saliva, or contaminated equipment. These diseases weaken the immune system and reduce milk production. Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD)
2 Bacterial Infections Bacteria enter the cow‚Äôs body through wounds, dirty udders, or contaminated food and water. They often cause severe infections if not treated early. Mastitis, Brucellosis
3 Parasitic Infections Parasites live on or inside the cow and feed on blood or nutrients, leading to weakness and poor growth. They are common in unhygienic and humid conditions. Ticks, Worms
4 Unhygienic Conditions Dirty sheds, wet floors, and accumulated waste create an ideal environment for bacteria and insects. This increases the risk of infections and skin diseases. Dirty shed, stagnant water
5 Sudden Feed Change Abrupt changes in fodder or diet disturb the digestive system of cows. This can lead to indigestion, bloating, and reduced nutrient absorption. Change in fodder
6 Nutritional Deficiency Lack of essential nutrients like calcium, phosphorus, and vitamins weakens bones and immunity. Nutritional imbalance directly affects milk yield and overall health. Calcium, minerals
7 Extreme Weather Excessive heat or cold causes stress in cows, lowering their immunity. Heat stress and cold stress increase susceptibility to infections. Heat waves, cold stress

4) Seasonal Diseases :

Season Common Diseases Seasonal Care
Rainy Season
  • Foot rot
  • Tick fever
  • Worm infection
  • Keep shed dry and clean
  • Use anti-tick spray regularly
  • Provide deworming as advised
  • Avoid muddy water areas
Winter Season
  • Pneumonia
  • Cold stress
  • Provide warm shelter and bedding
  • Avoid cold wind exposure
  • Give warm water if possible
  • Vaccinate and prevent overcrowding
Summer Season
  • Heat stroke
  • Dehydration
  • Provide shade and ventilation
  • Keep clean water always available
  • Give ORS / electrolytes if needed
  • Avoid grazing in peak afternoon

5) Do's and Dont's (CowCare Hub)

Do's

  • Provide clean drinking water
  • Keep shed clean and dry
  • Give balanced feed and minerals
  • Vaccinate cows regularly
  • Check udder health daily

Dont's

  • Do not keep sick cows with healthy cows
  • Do not suddenly change cow diet
  • Do not ignore fever and diarrhea
  • Do not use dirty milking equipment

6) Emergency Warning Signs


Important Notice
Contact a veterinarian immediately if the cow shows any of the following symptoms:
  • Cannot stand or becomes unconscious
  • Continuous high fever
  • Severe breathing problem
  • Blood in stool or urine
  • Severe dehydration
  • Sudden stop in milk production
