Government Schemes Supporting Cow Health and Farmers

Government schemes play an important role in supporting cow owners, farmers, and the dairy sector in India. These schemes are introduced by the government to improve cow health, protect indigenous breeds, increase milk production, and provide financial and technical support to farmers. Through these schemes, farmers receive assistance in healthcare, nutrition, breeding, and dairy development, which helps improve their livelihood and animal welfare.

IN FOCUS

National Dairy Development Programme (NDDP)

The main objective of the National Dairy Development Programme is to strengthen dairy infrastructure and improve milk production, processing, and marketing across the country.

Key Features

  • Development of milk collection and processing facilities
  • Strengthening dairy cooperatives and farmer institutions
  • Improving milk quality and storage systems
  • Support for organized dairy development

Benefits to Farmers

  • Better access to milk markets
  • Fair and stable income for dairy farmers
  • Reduced milk wastage
  • Improved milk quality and safety

Rashtriya Gokul Mission

The main objective of Rashtriya Gokul Mission is to develop and conserve indigenous cow breeds and improve their genetic quality for better milk production.

Key Features

  • Conservation of native cow breeds
  • Scientific breeding and genetic improvement
  • Establishment of Gokul Grams for indigenous cows
  • Use of modern breeding techniques

Benefits to Farmers

  • Improved milk yield from indigenous breeds
  • Better disease resistance in cows
  • Training and awareness for farmers
  • Long-term income support
